# v0.9 Launch Surface Controls Plan

Evidence makes Forge more grounded. The next step is to give the founder a few explicit controls that make the generated page closer to publishable.

The principle:

> Let Pendragon infer the surface, but let the founder control the offer.

## Product Bet

Forge should not force founders to edit a whole website builder. It should ask for the handful of details that cannot be safely inferred:

- what exactly is the offer?
- what should the primary CTA say?
- where should the CTA go?
- where should support point?
- what trust claim can the founder honestly make?
- what should the founder remember before publishing?

That keeps Pendragon lean while making the generated page feel less generic.

## v0.9 Scope

Add editable Forge controls:

- offer
- CTA label
- CTA URL
- support URL
- trust claim
- founder note

These controls are local-only and stored with the product room.

## Where Controls Appear

Forge uses controls in:

- in-app preview
- standalone HTML export
- CTA card
- offer section
- trust claim section
- support link
- founder note

## Non-Goals

Do not add:

- a visual page builder
- theme controls
- rich text editing
- publishing
- payments
- email capture
- analytics

Those belong later. v0.9 is only about giving Forge the minimum founder-owned details needed to generate a sharper draft.

## Writing Quest Dogfood Standard

Writing Quest needs:

- a concrete beta offer
- a paid-beta CTA label
- a trust claim that does not overpromise
- a reminder not to publish until save/export trust is real

If those four pieces are visible in the generated page, the module is doing useful launch work.

## Success Criteria

- Forge renders editable Launch Surface Controls.
- Saving controls updates the generated preview.
- Exported HTML includes the same offer, CTA, trust claim, support link, and founder note.
- Writing Quest starts with sensible control defaults.
- Invalid CTA/support URLs are rejected unless they are blank.
