# v0.10 Launch Surface QA Plan

Forge can now generate a launch surface and expose a few founder-owned controls. The next step is to judge whether that surface is actually fit to share.

The principle:

> A launch page is not useful until it can survive contact with a real buyer.

## Product Bet

Solo founders do not only need a generated page. They need a blunt read on whether the page is credible enough to publish.

Launch Surface QA gives Forge a narrow, practical review pass:

- is the offer explicit?
- does the CTA go somewhere real?
- is there proof attached?
- is there an honest trust claim?
- is there a support path?
- are critical docs assets still open?
- does the readiness gate allow a launch push?

This is intentionally smaller than a generic website audit. It is a founder-facing publishability check.

## v0.10 Scope

Add Launch Surface QA to Forge:

- publishability score
- shareable, caution, or blocked verdict
- seven plain-English checks
- clear-first list
- matching QA section in exported HTML

## Non-Goals

Do not add:

- automated SEO scoring
- design scoring
- performance scoring
- analytics
- external crawlers
- AI review
- publishing automation

Those are later products. v0.10 should only decide whether the generated page is ready to put in front of people.

## Writing Quest Dogfood Standard

Writing Quest should not be treated as shareable until:

- a real CTA URL exists
- critical docs assets are closed or downgraded
- the readiness gate clears
- proof remains attached to the launch surface

The page can still be useful before then, but Pendragon should say the quiet part out loud.

## Success Criteria

- Forge shows a Launch Surface QA block between controls and preview.
- QA status updates from the same product room data as the preview.
- Exported HTML includes the QA verdict and checklist.
- Writing Quest currently shows meaningful blockers instead of false confidence.
- The feature stays local-only and does not require connectors.
