# v0.13 Launch Surface History Plan

Forge can generate, score, package, and manually publish a launch surface. The next step is to remember what the surface looked like before the founder changes it.

The principle:

> A launch room should preserve the evolution of the launch story.

## Product Bet

Solo founders iterate quickly and forget why a launch page changed. That makes it hard to tell whether a new draft is stronger or just different.

Launch Surface History gives each product room:

- local launch surface snapshots
- current-vs-latest comparison
- QA score delta
- blocker delta
- proof-link delta
- docs-ready delta
- a short snapshot trail

This starts turning Pendragon into a launch system of record.

## v0.13 Scope

Add Launch Surface History to Forge:

- save the current generated surface as a local snapshot
- store up to ten snapshots per product
- show the latest snapshots in Forge
- compare the current generated surface against the latest saved snapshot
- keep snapshots local-only inside the workspace

## Non-Goals

Do not add:

- branching
- restore snapshot
- diff viewer
- snapshot deletion
- cloud sync
- team comments
- external version control

Those may matter later. v0.13 only proves that saved launch artifacts are valuable.

## Writing Quest Dogfood Standard

Writing Quest should be able to save a snapshot before we change the paid beta offer, CTA, trust claim, or docs status.

The current snapshot should make it obvious that:

- QA score is 57%
- CTA is still blocked
- critical docs remain open
- readiness gate remains blocked
- the publish folder is `launch/writing-quest/`

That gives us a baseline before we harden the beta launch room.

## Success Criteria

- Forge shows Launch Surface History after Manual Publish Path.
- Save Snapshot stores a local snapshot for the active product.
- The history list shows saved snapshots with time, verdict, score, surface type, and blockers.
- The comparison metrics update after saving.
- The workspace normalization preserves saved snapshots.
