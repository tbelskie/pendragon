# Writing Quest Product Brief

## One-Liner

Writing Quest is a retro, offline-first writing app that turns drafting into a private little adventure.

## User

Indie writers, hobbyists, and creative people who want a playful writing environment without internet-heavy productivity software.

## Problem

Writers want to draft consistently, but many writing tools feel sterile, distracting, overbuilt, or too connected to the internet.

## Promise

Make writing feel like a small quest: focused, tactile, private, and rewarding.

## Current Evidence

- Repo has meaningful product progress beyond skeleton.
- Core app loop exists: project creation, companion choice, editor, quests, autosave/recovery, export, project library, and staged companion evolution.
- NaNoWriMo-style November writing season creates launch timing, though the official brand should be avoided.
- Early testing already caught a blocking save-warning issue in browser preview, which has been fixed.
- Founder feedback clarified the art bar: lo-fi is acceptable, amateurish is not.
- Production art direction now exists in `tbelskie/writing-quest` after PR #29; execution is still open.

## Current Bet

Can a polished paid beta attract indie writers who want a focused, charming writing environment?

## Product Guardrails

- Make writers feel accompanied, not judged.
- Keep the game loop light and private.
- Prefer trust and clarity over novelty.
- Sell the writing ritual, not a productivity dashboard.
- Do not add internet-dependent behavior to the core experience.
