# Vision

Pendragon is the GTM warroom for micro-SaaS founders: one place to develop, document, and launch small software products with discipline.

It begins as an internal operating system for a four-product portfolio. The portfolio is the proof engine. Every workflow in Pendragon should be tested against real products, real docs, real launch assets, real store listings, real pricing decisions, and real retrospectives.

## Thesis

AI makes it easier than ever to build half a product. It does not automatically make that product coherent, documented, priced, positioned, launched, supported, or improved.

Micro-SaaS founders do not need another generic project manager. They need a launch room that turns scattered product work into a concrete path to market.

## Promise

Pendragon helps founders answer:

- What are we building?
- Who is it for?
- Why will they care?
- What are we charging?
- What must be documented?
- What must be fixed before launch?
- What channels are we testing?
- What evidence would prove this is working?
- What did we learn?

## Long-Term Product

The future Pendragon is a sleek web app plus Codex plugin.

The web app owns the warroom: products, launches, decisions, docs plans, GTM experiments, assets, checklists, retrospectives, and metrics.

The Codex plugin performs repo-native work: inspect a product repo, draft docs, audit launch readiness, generate issues, prepare changelogs, review UX copy, run checks, create PRs, and send structured findings back to Pendragon.

## Positioning

Working tagline:

> Develop, document, launch. The all-in-one GTM warroom for micro-SaaS founders.

Sharper variants to test:

- Stop shipping alone.
- Turn half-built projects into launched products.
- The launch room for founders without a GTM team.
- Build the product. Document the product. Launch the product.

## What Pendragon Is Not

- Not a generic AI business coach.
- Not a task manager with a chatbot bolted on.
- Not an idea generator.
- Not a replacement for judgment.
- Not a place to endlessly brainstorm instead of shipping.

Pendragon should create productive friction. It should force decisions, expose missing pieces, and move work toward launch.
